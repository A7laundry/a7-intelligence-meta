### 1. Triple Whale

**What it is:** E-commerce analytics platform trusted by 45,000+ brands, processing $65B+ in annual commerce data.

**Core Features:**
- Unified data platform pulling all business metrics into a single source of truth (real-time)
- Triple Pixel for proprietary attribution tracking
- Multi-Touch Attribution (MTA) + Marketing Mix Modeling (MMM) + live incrementality testing
- Creative Analysis dashboard: auto-analyzes creative designs, copy, and performance across Meta, Google, TikTok
- LLM/AI Search visibility tracking (via Anteater acquisition) - tracks brand visibility in AI chatbots

**What Makes It Unique:**
- Moby AI: purpose-built AI trained on billions of commerce events
- Moby Agents: autonomous agents that spot opportunities or flag anomalies before they impact revenue
- Moby Artifacts: generates presentation-ready reports, documents, visualizations from chat
- Workspaces: organized dashboards and agents around specific jobs for contextual insights
- AI embedded directly into Summary, Attribution, and other views

**Key Modules:**
- Summary Dashboard (AI-enhanced)
- Attribution Dashboard (MTA + MMM)
- Creative Analysis Dashboard
- Profit Tracking (profit-first vs. traffic-first analytics)
- Forecasting
- Moby AI Chat Interface
- Moby Agents (autonomous)

**Meta Ads Handling:**
- Deep Meta integration via Triple Pixel
- Creative performance analysis by ad, ad set, campaign
- Channel budget reallocation recommendations
- Generates creative briefs and audience suggestions

**Dashboard Pattern:** Real-time KPI cards + AI-embedded insights inline with each view + chat-based AI assistant

**What it is:** Server-side ad tracking and attribution platform with AI remarketing.

**Core Features:**

- Deep Mode: drill into individual customer journeys (every click, page visit, touchpoint)
- AI-powered link attribution (Feb 2026): traces every click and conversion across entire customer journey

**What Makes It Unique:**
- Hyros AIR (AI Remarketing): identifies known and anonymous visitors, learns preferences, automatically re-engages through personalized communication
- 15% average increase in ad ROI from tracking alone
- 3-7% more revenue from AI remarketing
- Parallel tracking system running alongside Meta and Google (independent verification)

**Key Modules:**
- Attribution Dashboard (multi-model)
- Customer Journey Deep Mode
- AIR Remarketing Engine
- Link Attribution Tracking
- Revenue Reporting

**Meta Ads Handling:**
- Server-side tracking parallel to Meta's own reporting

**Dashboard Pattern:** Campaign-level performance tables + customer journey drill-downs + attribution model switcher

## Part 4: Key Architectural Patterns Observed

### 1. AI-First, Not AI-Added
The best platforms (Triple Whale, Madgicx) embed AI directly INTO every view, not as a separate tab. Moby AI appears inline in the Attribution dashboard, in the Summary, in Creative Analysis. The AI is the interface.

### 2. Action-Oriented, Not Just Informational
Madgicx's one-click optimizations and Madgicx's autonomous budget optimizer show that the winning pattern is: **insight + action in the same place**. Don't just show "ROAS is dropping" -- show a button that says "Pause underperforming ad sets" or "Shift $50/day to top performer."

### 3. Visual-Creative-First for Creative Analytics
Motion proved that creative analytics must show the actual creative (thumbnail, video preview) alongside metrics. Tables of numbers are not enough for creative decisions.

### 4. Agentic Architecture
Both Triple Whale (Moby Agents) and Madgicx (AI Marketer) are moving toward autonomous agents that continuously monitor and act. The future is not dashboards you check -- it's agents that come to you with findings and proposed actions.

### 5. Server-Side Tracking as Table Stakes
Hyros and Madgicx both emphasize server-side tracking as essential for accurate Meta Ads attribution in the post-iOS 14.5 world. This is no longer a differentiator -- it's expected.

### 6. Profit Over Revenue
Triple Whale and Northbeam both emphasize profit tracking over revenue tracking. The shift is from "how much did we make?" to "how much did we actually keep?"
